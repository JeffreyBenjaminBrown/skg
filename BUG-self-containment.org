* BUG: self-containment in TypeDB after merging container into content

** Summary
When merging a container into one of its own content nodes,
the acquirer ends up containing itself in TypeDB.
The filesystem is correct — the bug is only in TypeDB.

** Reproduction
Test: cargo test --test merge merge_container_into_content -- --nocapture

Fixture graph:
  a contains [aa, b, c]
  aa contains [x]

Buffer merges a into aa (editRequest (merge a) on aa).

After merge:
- Filesystem: aa.contains = [preserver, x, b, c] (correct, 4 items)
- TypeDB: aa contains {aa} (self-containment!)

The old a→aa containment relationship wasn't cleaned up;
it got reassigned to aa (via extra_id inheritance from a).

** Symptom
The rendered buffer shows (contents 5) in aa's graphStats,
but aa only has 4 actual content children.
The 5th is aa itself, counted from the TypeDB self-containment.

** Where to look
The merge logic is in the save pipeline.
The TypeDB update presumably needs to remove the contains relationship
from the acquiree to the acquirer before reassigning extra_ids.
