* Codex's work
** hypotheses
*** Rerender, not filesystem, was losing the bodies.
The regression test reproduced the body loss in the saved view,
but direct reads of =b1.skg= and =b2.skg= after the merge still
showed =body: b1 text= and =body: b2 text=.

That ruled out the merge filesystem write as the immediate cause.
The problem was in rebuilding the saved buffer after the graph had
already been updated.
*** Missing children created during rerender were bodyless.
During saved-view rerender, =complete_content_children= creates
children that are in the graph's =contains= list but missing under
the current view node.

It fetched title and source from disk, but it did not carry the
body into the newly-created definitive =ViewNode=:
it called =mk_definitive_viewnode (..., None)=.

In this bug, after merging =b= into =a=, =b1= and =b2= were missing
under the definitive =a= branch and got created that way.
** observations
*** Reproduction
I added and first ran:

: cargo test --test merge merge_preserves_acquiree_child_bodies -- --nocapture

Before the fix, it failed.  The rendered buffer contained:

: ** (skg (node (id b1) (source main))) b1
: ** (skg (node (id b2) (source main))) b2

with no following =b1 text= or =b2 text= body lines.

The same test also read =b1= and =b2= from disk after the merge,
and those bodies were still present.  Therefore the bug reproduced,
but as a saved-view rendering bug rather than a data-loss-on-disk bug.
*** Why this was merge-specific
The merge rerender rewrites the acquiree root =b= as another view of
the acquirer =a=, then reconciles =a='s content list from the updated
graph.  The adopted acquiree children are now graph-children of =a=,
but they were not children of the original =a= branch in the incoming
viewforest.

That made them newly-created rerender children, which exposed the
body-dropping path.
** methods
*** Test
I added a regression test at:

: tests/merge/merge_preserves_acquiree_child_bodies.rs

with fixtures in:

: tests/merge/merge_preserves_acquiree_child_bodies/fixtures/

The test merges =b= into =a= where =b1= and =b2= are children of =b=
and both have bodies.  It asserts both:

- the bodies remain on disk after the merge
- the saved view contains those body lines after rerender
*** Fix
I extended =ChildData= in
=server/update_buffer/complete_preorder/truenode.rs= to include
=body: Option<String>=.

For real children fetched from disk, =build_child_creation_data=
now stores =skg.body.clone()=.  =complete_content_children= passes
that body to =mk_definitive_viewnode= instead of passing =None=.

Phantom children still use =body: None=.
