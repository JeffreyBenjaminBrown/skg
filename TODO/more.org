* Why does this search fail?
** Searched "rt"-style for
"dens.* AND (mean.* OR info.* OR know.*)"
** got this error
Error searching index: An invalid argument was passed: 'RegexQueryError: regex parse error:
    (mean.*
    ^
error: unclosed group'
** TODO Claude : answer -- regex mode has no operator-level grouping
In regex+operators mode ('build_regex_query',
server/dbs/tantivy/search.rs), the query is split on WHITESPACE; the
keywords AND / OR / NOT and the +/- prefixes are consumed as
operators, and EVERY OTHER piece must be a valid regex on its own.
Your "(mean.*" is such a piece: the "(" opens a regex group that
never closes, hence Tantivy's RegexQueryError. Parentheses as
operator-level grouping exist only on the non-regex QueryParser path
(plain "t"-style with operators); the regex path never got a grouping
layer.

You don't need one for this query, though: regexes have alternation
natively, so grouping can live INSIDE a single piece. This does what
you meant:

  dens.* AND (mean|info|know).*

(one piece per required token; both pieces adjacent to AND become
MUST). If you want operator-level parens in regex mode anyway --
e.g. for mixing MUST_NOT into a group -- say so; it needs a small
boolean-expression parser over the pieces in
'regex_pieces_with_operators', where today's grammar is flat.
