Most of this is straightforward,
but the super-indentation rules are not.

SUPER-INDENTATION
means a node has org children at more than one headline depth, e.g. *** child directly under a * parent alongside ** child or * child levels. The importer does not preserve that as raw skipped org levels. Instead, bottom-up, it inserts synthetic grouping nodes whenever a node’s children span multiple headline levels.

For each such mixed-level child set:

- The most-indented children go under a generated node titled These are special!.
- That generated node’s body is They were super-indented in org-roam.
- The remaining, less-indented children go under a generated node titled These are normal.
- That node’s body says they were buried to encourage reading the super-indented nodes.
- If the “normal” remainder still contains multiple child levels, the same split happens recursively.
- Synthetic grouping nodes get fresh UUIDs later, just like imported org headings without :ID:.
- Original child subtrees are kept under whichever grouping node they land in.

So for levels 4, 3, and 2 under the same parent, the importer groups level 4 as “special”, then recursively groups levels 3 and 2 inside “normal”: level 3 becomes another “special”, and level 2 becomes
another “normal”.

One nuance from the code: Grouping is by headline level, not by contiguous runs. All children at the deepest direct level are partitioned into the special group together, and all others into the normal group. The linked note on GitHub frames the intent as preserving the reading signal of “pre-content” while not preserving the exact number of asterisks as meaningful measure:
