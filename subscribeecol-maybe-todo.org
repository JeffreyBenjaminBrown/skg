* Possible collateral rerender bug in SubscribeeCol

There might be a stale-view danger in how SubscribeeCol children are
reconciled during collateral rerender.

Suppose two buffers are open.

Buffer 1 is the one the user saves:

#+begin_src org
* subscriber {id: S, source: main}
** subscribee column {kind: subscribeeCol}
*** old target {id: A, source: main, definitive: false}
*** new target {id: B, source: main, definitive: false}
#+end_src

Buffer 2 is another open view containing the same subscriber:

#+begin_src org
* subscriber {id: S, source: main}
** subscribee column {kind: subscribeeCol}
*** old target {id: A, source: main, definitive: false}
#+end_src

After Buffer 1 saves, the graph says:

#+begin_src org
* subscriber {id: S, source: main, subscribes-to: B}
#+end_src

The collateral rerender of Buffer 2 should become:

#+begin_src org
* subscriber {id: S, source: main}
** subscribee column {kind: subscribeeCol}
*** new target {id: B, source: main, definitive: false}
#+end_src

The concern is in 'complete_subscribee_col_preorder'. It reads the
fresh 'subscribes_to' list and computes a goal list, but in non-diff
mode it only reconciles the SubscribeeCol's children when the
subscriber is indefinitive:

#+begin_src text
if parent_indefinitive || source_diffs.is_some() { reconcile children }
#+end_src

That condition makes sense for the saved view: a definitive subscriber
in the saved buffer is the user's source of truth, so the children
under its SubscribeeCol define what should be saved.

It is suspicious for collateral views. A definitive subscriber in a
collateral buffer is not defining the current save. It is stale UI
that should be refreshed from the graph, just like title, body, source,
and content children. If the condition is applied unchanged during a
collateral rerender, a definitive subscriber's SubscribeeCol might keep
old children even though the graph's 'subscribes_to' field has changed.

This needs a focused test before changing behavior. The test should
open two views containing the same subscriber, change its subscriptions
in one view, save, and assert that the other view's SubscribeeCol
matches the saved graph.
